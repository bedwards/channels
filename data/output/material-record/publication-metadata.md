## Short Description (Substack "About" — one paragraph)

Material Record publishes data-driven long-form essays on political economy, institutional power, and the structural forces that produce headlines without appearing in them. The writing starts from specific numbers — trust fund projections, defense budgets, tax receipts, vote totals — and works outward toward the questions those numbers raise. We draw on political economy, democratic theory, neuroscience, and historical precedent. We hold both parties accountable with receipts. We do not preach. We do not simplify. We sit with the complexity and try to describe what we see.

## Extended About

Material Record is an essay publication that treats current events as symptoms of deeper structural patterns.

Every piece starts from source material — journalists, historians, researchers doing the primary work — and uses it as a springboard. Not a summary. Not a reaction. A reframing. What does this event look like when you step far enough back to see the mechanism?

The method is data-first. We present the numbers before making claims. We cite sources inline. We name the measurement problems — the gaps between what our instruments detect and what is actually happening. When we say something is complicated, we mean it's actually complicated.

We are anti-war, arrived at through evidence rather than posture. We believe in distributed power and skepticism toward concentrated authority, whether state or corporate. We believe the neuroscience on selfhood complicates the assumptions that classical liberalism takes as foundational. We don't argue for these positions. They're the lens we look through.

Sources include Heather Cox Richardson, Novara Media, and whatever else illuminates the structure beneath the event.

Published daily. No sponsors. No algorithm optimization.

Part of the Lluminate Network.
